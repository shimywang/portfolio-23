Explorii: Detouring Route Planner for Helsinki Public Transportation

Service Design, User Experience, Journey Planner

01.2018 - 05.2018

Espoo, Finland

Design Project Course Project

This is a course project collaborating with HSL(Helsinki Regional Transport Authority), focusing on finding out problems people have related to public transportation and provide design solutions.

Collaborated with Lena Hegemann, Juho Saari, Son Nguyen and Elli-Mari Hakkarainen

1 Finding out problems

Unlike many other cities, Helsinki does not have typical problem with public transportation such as traffic jams or unreliable time tables.

To better understand what problem people are having with public transportation, we tried multiple ways to target problems to solve:

One of our team member noticed that there has been comments regarding health issues Finnish have of lack of exercise from local press.

We also found out 3 main problems that are interesting for us from our interviews & surveys.

1. Mental efforts with new routes

When traveling with new routes, travelers need to spend extra mental efforts on finding out where is the bus stops or which direction it is going.

2. Lack of services outside core routes

If you are travelling outside the downtown Helsinki, buses might come every 30 mins.

3. Time spent on buses

Frequent travelers spend a reasonalbe time on buses, while they feel like they didn’t utilize this part of time.

We dicided to focus on combining general needs of getting more excercise with public transportation.

2 How to combine exercising with public transportation

We used 6-3-5 methods for brainstomring ideas to combine exercising with public transportation, that could also motivate people for exercising more and generated 50+ original ideas.

After analyzing and categorizing ideas, we collected 16 ideas for combining exercising with public transportation, and then created value-proposition card for each of the idea for collecting feedback.

To evaluate these generated ideas and choose one to continue, we created Value-Proposition Cards, with an image describing the general idea and one sentence summarizing how does the idea solve user problems.

We created value-proposition card for each of our idea, and interviewed 16 users, asking them to pick three of the ideas they like most.

Based on interview result, we found out that users in general prefer idea that supports users to explore the surrounding environments, which motivate them walk more.

3 Design Concept

Our final design solution for combining public transportation with exercising is a route planner that help users to detour on their routes. We noticed that while taking buses and metro, there is always a 10 - 15 mins gap when transferring. One comment we collected from users is that "I want to do something else rather then just standing and waiting at the bus stop."

Based on this, we designed a route planner that detour users for a 10 - 15 minutes journey on their routes. Users could be detoured to enjoy the beautiful view of Helsinki that is around them, or going for a trip to a cafeteria or grocery shop to fill up this 10 - 15 mins time gap.

We named it Explorii, as it is aiming at helping citizens and frequent travlers to explore the unknown part of the city to motivate them exercise more.

We designed two business models for Explorii(as part of our course requirement):
(1) Detouring users to advertised locations;
(2) Provide customizable options and detailed description for Premium users.
After developing the main design concept and two optional business models, we tested out our ideas by getting feedback from prototypes.

4 Evaluate with Prototypes

Does detouring work?

We conducted two rounds of evaluation for our idea, first round in central area of Helsinki and second round in less crowded area in Lauttasaari to see how well the idea of detouring work.

As we are mostly aiming at to test out whether the idea work or not, we created a quick-and-dirty prototype with Google Maps and send instructions to users using Line.

We used Wizard of Oz’ method to carry out tests with participants. To achieve this, one instructor would be observing and sending message to participants. Another person will also be recording and making notes throughout the journey.

We evaluated our prototype with 3 participants who lives in Helsinki and has experienced with using public transportation services in Helsinki.

In Less-crowded areas

To figure out how Explorii works, we condcuted our second test in less crowded, and less touristic area in Lautasaari.

We also added two features to our prototype:
(1) Crowdsoursing
Users can upload their own POIs and add into Explorii, to solve the problems of lack of POIs in less crowded areas.

2) Opstional Routes
Users can choose what route they are interested in.

Similarily, we tested with 4 users who lives in Helsinki and has experience with using public transportation services.
We offered two routes for users to choose, one with a small and nice cafe in Lautasarii, the other one with beautiful natural view on Lautassari island.

Additionally, we asked participants to stimulate crowdsourcing with extra tasks. Participants are required to take photo during the trip and send it back to the system.
