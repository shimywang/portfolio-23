Scenario Planning: How would mobility services become in 2035?

Scenario Planning, Design Fiction, Future Mobility

2021

Shanghai,China

An internal project to explore how mobility services in the far future, showcase our creativity & ability to potential clients.

1 Background: An exploration to future mobility services

This project was launched at the end of 2021, when it became very clear that EVs (electric vehicles) might soon replace fuel cars in the near future. However, there wasn’t a clear answer of how EVs will be and how mobility will become.

It made us wonder:
How would vehicles become in the future?

-Will it remain to be something very similar to fuel cars?
-Or, will it transfer into something completely different?

This is an internal project, aiming to showcase our future clients of using novel methodologies to explore even further future, and provide brand new insights and inspirations to the industry, which might support product strategy in a longer run and generate innovative solutions.

2 Methodology

To build up in-depth future scenarios, we used scenario planning, scenario building and design fiction.

By scenario planning, we get multiple predictions of the future marco society, which we extend to a set of scenarios that represent multiple different possible futures. In order to help reader to better emphasize the possible future world, we use scenario building to create future scenarios &future personas, illustrating future world in micro level with more details.

With descriptions of future scenarios from both marco and micro level, we emphasize and analyze possible opportunities for future mobility products. Besides, we also use design fiction to create vivid stories on how future mobility products/services could be used, which are used for illustrate their key values and a tool for further decision-making.

3 Process

01 Starting from presents, collect future drives

We used PESTEL Model to collect current trends. After analyzing these collected trends, we determined future drives, that might impact the organization and its future decision. Below are the six dimensions of trends from PESTEL model.

Below are two samples of our collected future drives.

SOCIAL: Diversification of family structure, emerging new relationship of living together

In future, traditional family might be replaced by a new form of cohabitation relationship. Living together does not mean that you need to get married or involved in any further relationship, but simply just a companion for each other, playing together and sharing risks.

There are multiple reasons for such non-traditional cohabitation, such as to reduce risk, or the desire of getting connected to someone else.

ECONOMIC: Low desire society is coming. Future young generation will have lower desire and ability to consume

With frequent natural disaster, world-wide pandemic and turbulent international situation, it is clear that the global economic will move to a downturn. Such external influences will further cause the consumption ability and desire of future young generation fall further, and therefore drastically affect future consumption habits.

Furthermore, the aging of Chinese society is also speeding up. It is predicted that in 2030, aging group of 35-44, and a more senior group of above 55, would be the main consumers in China. On the other hand, the proportion of total consumption from young people under 35 would drop significantly.

02 Scenario planning: from present to future

We analyzed collected future drives based on the following 2 dimensions:

01/Uncertainty

How certain is it to happen within 10 years?

02/Critical

How critical it could affect the problem we focusing on?

We filtered out key drives that are uncertain and critical.

Based on these key drives, we invited specialists from various fields, to review add on, and select pairs of critical uncertainties as possible directions to determine future scenario.

03 Scenario building& Persona building

We dived deep into each future scenario, add detail to these scenarios by generating future scenario stories and future persona stories that we consider typical and inspiring under each scenario.

04 Future product & product opportunity

After having enriched details towards each scenario, we abstracted product developing space for future mobility products based on future personas and future scenario stories. We also imagined how future product and service could be under each scenario, and developed detailed description of future tools using design fiction.

4 Outcome

01 Future Scenarios

We generated 4 typical and representative future scenarios based on future drives.

02 Future scenario story

We create multiple scenario stories that are easy to read and empathize with vivid details. Below are two samples of our future scenario stories.

Back to Villa being a new civil trend

Over the past 30 years, the increasing influx of people into cities has made urban personal living space even more cramped and tiny.

The new countryside, with its natural beauty and convenient public services, has become the most attractive place to live for city dwellers.

The new generations are moving away from their cities. They see villages as the new trending place, with large space and modern facilities, yet diverse natural landscapes.





Young people reject rat race and seek for inner peace

The new generation finally burned out in 2035. These young people has find out that, no matter how hard they work, most people can only get an average job at an average pay. Therefore, it is not worthy to live an old life as their previous generations - worked hard and get a promising future, as there is no such promises anymore.

Most young people will consider work to be just work, as there is almost no hope of getting promoted or increased salary by simply devoting to works.

Therefore, youths has chosen to lower their expectation in their career, that they don’t expect to make a lot of money, but rather consider ‘am I happy or not with this job’ as their key career criteria.
