WeBrew: IoT solution for home brewing beer

IoT, Service Design, User Experience

09.2017 - 05.2018

Espoo, Finland

PDP Course Project

Sponsored by Ericsson

Beer has long been expensive in Nordic countries and thus many beer lovers in Finland start to brew their own beer at home. We want to seek for how to better support home brewing with IoT techniques. This is a course project sponsored by Ericcson.

Collaborated with Hazal Alkan, Sami Vaholuoto, Waleed Iftikhar, Hongyu Zhang, Tharun Roshan Kumar, Jani Canth, John Mulclay, and Niranjan Wad

Problems

We found out 3 main problems users mainly have from our interviews and online questionnaires:

1. Home brewing is labor-intensive
2. Fermentation is hard to control
3. Lack of support for customizing recipes

We decided to focus on the problems users have with fermentation and customizing recipes. These are the two main problems users are having. Also, there is already products in the market that are fully automated for solving the first problem.

Exisiting Products

We explored other existing products, and decided to build our solution to be not fully manually, but also not fully automated to enable our users to enjoy the process of grafting beers.

The final design is divided into hardware part and software part.
For the hardware part we are aiming at designing a vessle that users can used for fermentation, with which users can control fermentation temperature and monitor fermentation process.

For the software part we want to build a mobile APP that help users with modifying their recipes, recording batches and controling and monitoring whole fermentation process.
I was mainly focusing on the software side, and we did three iteration for the mobile interface.
We designed the mobile application based on common home brewing scenarios and home brewing tasks extracted from interviews and questionnaires. During our iteration, we evaluated our prototype with both beginner as well as professional users and iterated our design based on the feedback.

3 Design of Mobile APP

When designing mobile APP, we aimed at deliver an “All-in-one” experience to our end users, meaning that they can do everything they need when home brewing, from searching for and creating own recipes to monitoring brewing process.
Mobile APP has three main features:

1. Discovery new recipes shared by others

Users can search for recipes by two ways: they can search directly by name if they have a clear idea of what they want to find, or by types of beer or features of their dream beer.

Searching result will be shown in a card as below:

Clicking the result card will go to the recipe page, which shows ingredients you should prepare and instructions of brewing.

2. Monitor ongoing bataches

While brewing, users can get step-by-step instructions on Mashing and Boiling process. When it comes to fermentation, users can control fermentation temperature and monitor alcohol contents remotely.

Users can see more detailed information by clicking into the batch.
As fermentation is a long process and in most cases users have decided fermentation temperature and time, it is less likely that users will change fermentation temperature in the middle of fermentation process. However, users could change temperature and fermentation time by editing the recipe.

3. Viewing Histroy brews

After brewing users could find previous brew in History panel, leave comments and rate for the recipe and based on this to further modify their own recipes.

4. Create recipe lists and create own recipes

Users can create lists for collecting recipes, create own recipes and modify existing recipes.

Creating recipe lists could help users managing their favourite home brewing recipes and planning future brews.

5. Dark mode

We also designed dark mode for our mobile app.

Reflection

As we were developing this project in a short period, there are many things we didn’t think of. We were mainly focusing on the fermentation part. Although users could create and modify recipes on our mobile app, it is a long and complicated process. Also, user needs such as creating grocery list and sharing recipes with friends are not covered by our current design.

I was also thinking about improving interaction with the hardware part, as when brewing beer users are still mainly interacting with the machine instead of mobile APP, but we didn’t got that many time in exploring interaction with the hardware.
